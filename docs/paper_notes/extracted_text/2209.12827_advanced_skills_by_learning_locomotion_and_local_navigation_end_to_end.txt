                                            Abstract— The common approach for local navigation
                                         on challenging environments with legged robots requires
                                         path planning, path following and locomotion, which usually
                                         requires a locomotion control policy that accurately tracks
                                         a commanded velocity. However, by breaking down the

                                         complete problem by training an end-to-end policy with deep
                                         reinforcement learning. Instead of continuously tracking a
                                         precomputed path, the robot needs to reach a target position
                                         within a provided time. The task’s success is only evaluated at
                                         the end of an episode, meaning that the policy does not need to
                                         reach the target as fast as possible. It is free to select its path
